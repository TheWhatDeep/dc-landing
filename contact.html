Quick answers

Frequently asked questions.

How is Digital Circuitry different from a typical VA agency?

We're not an agency — we're a workers' cooperative under Philippine law (RA 9520). That means our member-VAs are part-owners of the business, with voting rights, profit-sharing through surplus distribution, and full benefits. Agencies extract value from VAs; we share it with them.

Do I need to be in the Philippines to hire from you?

Not at all. Our VAs are Filipino, but our clients are global. We work with businesses across the US, UK, Australia, Canada, and beyond. All time zones supported — just let us know your preferred working hours.

What happens if my VA becomes unavailable?

We identify a replacement within 3–5 business days. A 2-week transition period lets the outgoing VA brief the incoming one. You don't pay for the transition. This is one of our biggest advantages over hiring individual freelancers.

How much does it cost to hire a VA?

Our pricing depends on the VA tier you need. General VAs start at around ₱40,000/month, Executive VAs at ₱80,000, and Specialized VAs at ₱150,000–₱200,000+ depending on expertise. All pricing includes full benefits coverage for the VA and our cooperative's operational costs.

I'm a Filipino VA — how do I apply?

Fill out the form above and select "I want to join." Submit your resume and portfolio. We'll review your application, conduct a skills assessment and interview, and if it's a good fit, invite you to our pre-membership orientation. We respond personally to every applicant.

Do I have to pay anything to become a member?

No upfront payment is required. Your capital contribution — which establishes your ownership stake — is automatically accumulated from a small slice of the cooperative's operational fees over time. You never pay anything out of pocket.

What's the time commitment for becoming a member?

After acceptance, there's a 3–6 month probationary period where you work with your first client. During this time, benefits start immediately. After successful probation, you become a full member with voting rights, HMO coverage, and surplus eligibility.
