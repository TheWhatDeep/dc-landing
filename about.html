About us

A different way to work, together.

Digital Circuitry was built on a simple idea: the people doing the work should also own the work. We are a Workers' Cooperative under Philippine law, designed for the realities of remote work in 2026.

Most Filipino Virtual Assistants exist in a precarious in-between space — too informal to access SSS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG, or HMO coverage, but too professional to be treated like hobbyists. They build careers, support families, and contribute meaningfully to businesses worldwide — yet they often do so without the basic protections every worker deserves.

We started Digital Circuitry to change that. Not by being another agency that takes a cut and moves on — but by building a true cooperative where the VAs themselves are the owners. Every member has a vote in how the cooperative is run. Every member receives full government-mandated benefits and HMO coverage. Every member shares in the surplus when the cooperative thrives.

"The cooperative model has existed in the Philippines since 1990 under RA 6938, refined in 2008 under RA 9520. What's new is applying it to remote knowledge work."

This isn't a startup pitch or a tech disruption story. This is a centuries-old idea — that workers can own the means of their own labor — applied to the most modern form of work there is.

Our name, Digital Circuitry, reflects what we believe: that the digital world runs on real people, connected to each other in real networks. Every node matters. Every connection counts. And when one piece of the circuit thrives, the whole system gets stronger.

What we believe

Five values that guide every decision.

01 / Ownership

Workers own the work.

Every full member is a co-owner of the cooperative — with voting rights, surplus shares, and a real stake in how things are run.

02 / Dignity

Real benefits, not perks.

SSS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG, and HMO are not optional add-ons. They are the baseline of treating workers with dignity.

03 / Democracy

One person, one vote.

Regardless of income, tenure, or capital contribution — every member has an equal voice in the cooperative's direction.

04 / Transparency

Open books, open decisions.

Members can access the cooperative's financial records. No hidden margins, no opaque governance.

05 / Quality

Excellence as a shared standard.

When everyone owns the outcome, everyone protects the quality. Our clients feel the difference.

06 / Community

A portion always goes back.

5% of every peso of surplus funds community development initiatives — required by law, embraced by us.

The Legal Foundation

Digital Circuitry is registered as a Workers' Cooperative under Republic Act No. 9520 — the Philippine Cooperative Code of 2008 — and supervised by the Cooperative Development Authority (CDA).

This legal structure grants the cooperative its own juridical personality, separate from its members. Members enjoy limited liability, democratic governance protections, and certain tax incentives unique to cooperatives under Philippine law.

Most importantly, this structure is recognized internationally as a legitimate form of business — meaning clients anywhere in the world can engage us with full confidence in our legal standing.
